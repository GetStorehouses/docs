## Overview

Track your luxury watch collection with detailed fields for brand, model, reference numbers, movement types, and service history. Perfect for collectors, investors, and enthusiasts managing high-end timepieces.

<Card title="Watch Tracking Features" icon="clock">
  - Brand and model documentation
  - Reference number database
  - Movement type tracking
  - Case material and size
  - Box and papers status
  - Service history
  - Market value tracking
</Card>

## Adding a Watch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Add Item">
    Click "Add Item" from your dashboard or go to [storehouses.app/add](https://storehouses.app/add)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Luxury Watches">
    Choose "Luxury Watches" as the category
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Watch Details">
    Fill in brand, model, reference number, and specifications
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Documentation">
    Upload photos of watch, box, papers, and certificates
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click "Save Item" to add to your collection
  </Step>
</Steps>

  <Tip>

<ParamField path="model" type="string">
  Specific model or collection name

  **Examples:**
  - Submariner Date
  - Nautilus
  - Royal Oak
  - Speedmaster Professional
  - Daytona
  - Seamaster
  - Carrera
  - Portugieser

  <Note>
    Include the full model name including any sub-model designations
  </Note>
</ParamField>

### Reference and Serial Numbers

<ParamField path="reference_number" type="string">
  Official reference number

  **Examples:**
  - Rolex: 126610LN (Submariner Date)
  - Patek Philippe: 5711/1A-010 (Nautilus)
  - Omega: 310.30.42.50.01.001 (Speedmaster)
  - Audemars Piguet: 15500ST.OO.1220ST.01 (Royal Oak)

  **Use:** Critical for:
  - Accurate identification
  - Market value research
  - Insurance documentation
  - Resale purposes
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="serial_number" type="string">
  Unique serial number

  **Location varies by brand:**
  - Between lugs
  - On case back
  - On movement
  - Certificate/papers

  <Warning>
    Serial numbers are sensitive information. Keep photos private and secure.
  </Warning>
</ParamField>

### Movement and Technical Specifications

<ParamField path="movement_type" type="select">
  Type of watch movement

  **Options:**
  - **Automatic** - Self-winding mechanical movement
  - **Manual** - Hand-wound mechanical movement
  - **Quartz** - Battery-powered electronic movement
  - **Spring Drive** - Seiko's hybrid technology
  - **Smartwatch** - Electronic smartwatch
  - **Other** - Other movement types

  <Note>
    Most luxury watches are automatic or manual mechanical movements
  </Note>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="caliber" type="string">
  Movement caliber or reference

  **Examples:**
  - Rolex: 3235, 4130
  - Omega: 8800, 9300
  - Patek Philippe: 324 S C
  - ETA: 2824-2, 7750

  **Use:** Identifies specific movement used in the watch
</ParamField>

### Case Details

<ParamField path="case_material" type="string">
  Material of watch case

  **Common materials:**
  - Stainless Steel (most common)
  - Gold (yellow, white, rose)
  - Platinum
  - Titanium
  - Ceramic
  - Two-tone (steel and gold)
  - Bronze
  - Carbon fiber

  **Example entries:**
  - "Stainless Steel"
  - "18K Yellow Gold"
  - "Platinum 950"
  - "Grade 2 Titanium"
  - "Two-Tone Stainless Steel and Rose Gold"
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="case_size" type="string">
  Case diameter and thickness

  **Examples:**
  - "40mm"
  - "42mm × 12mm"
  - "44mm diameter, 15.5mm thick"

  **Standard sizes:**
  - 36-38mm: Traditional/vintage sizing
  - 40-42mm: Modern standard
  - 44mm+: Large/oversized watches

  <Tip>
    Include both diameter and thickness when known
  </Tip>
</ParamField>

### Production and Purchase Details

<ParamField path="year_produced" type="number">
  Year the watch was manufactured

  **Examples:**
  - 2024
  - 2020
  - 1990
  - 1965

  **Use:** Important for:
  - Vintage watch documentation
  - Warranty periods
  - Model year variations
  - Collector value
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="year_purchased" type="number">
  Year you acquired the watch

  **Use:** Track ownership history and holding period
</ParamField>

### Condition and Service

<ParamField path="condition" type="select">
  Overall condition of the watch

  **Options:**
  - **New/Unworn** - Never worn, with stickers
  - **Excellent** - Minimal wear, like new
  - **Very Good** - Light wear, well maintained
  - **Good** - Normal wear, good condition
  - **Fair** - Visible wear, fully functional
  - **Poor** - Heavy wear or needs service

  <Note>
    Condition significantly affects market value
  </Note>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="service_history" type="textarea">
  Service and maintenance records

  **Include:**
  - Date of service
  - Service provider (authorized dealer/independent)
  - Work performed
  - Cost
  - Any replaced parts

  **Example:**
  ```
  June 2023 - Full service by Rolex Service Center
  - Movement overhaul
  - Replaced crown seals
  - Pressure test passed
  - Cost: $850

  March 2020 - Bracelet adjustment at AD
  - Removed 2 links
  - Polished clasp
  ```

  <Tip>
    Regular servicing maintains value and longevity. Keep all service receipts.
  </Tip>
</ParamField>

### Box and Papers

<ParamField path="box_papers" type="select">
  Completeness of original packaging and documentation

  **Options:**
  - **Full Set** - Watch, box, papers, warranty card, everything
  - **Box and Papers** - Complete with documents
  - **Papers Only** - Documentation but no box
  - **Box Only** - Original box but no papers
  - **Watch Only** - No box or papers

  <Note>
    Complete sets (box and papers) typically command higher resale values
  </Note>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="warranty_status" type="string">
  Current warranty status

  **Examples:**
  - "Active - expires December 2028"
  - "Expired - original 5-year warranty"
  - "No warranty - vintage piece"
  - "Transferable warranty valid until 2026"

  <Tip>
    Some brands offer transferable warranties. Keep warranty cards safe.
  </Tip>
</ParamField>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Document Everything" icon="file-text">
    Create complete records including:
    - All reference numbers (watch and movement)
    - Serial numbers
    - Year of production
    - Purchase documentation
    - Service records
    - Box and papers status

    **Why:** Essential for insurance, resale, and authenticity verification
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Photograph Thoroughly" icon="camera">
    Take detailed photos of:
    - Full watch face
    - Case back (if visible)
    - Side profile showing case thickness
    - Clasp and bracelet
    - Serial numbers (between lugs)
    - Movement (if exhibition case back)
    - Box and papers
    - Certificate/warranty card
    - Any unique features or flaws

    <Tip>
      Use good lighting and capture sharp, clear images for insurance purposes
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Track Service History" icon="wrench">
    Maintain detailed service records:
    - Service date and provider
    - Work performed
    - Parts replaced
    - Cost of service
    - Next service due date

    **Service intervals:**
    - Rolex: Every 10 years (or 5 years for older models)
    - Omega: Every 5-8 years
    - Most mechanical watches: Every 3-5 years

    <Note>
      Regular servicing maintains value and ensures longevity
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Keep Box and Papers" icon="box">
    Original packaging increases value:
    - **Full set** (watch, box, papers): Highest value
    - **Box and papers**: Strong resale value
    - **Papers only**: Certificate of authenticity
    - **Watch only**: Lower resale value

    **Papers include:**
    - Warranty card
    - Certificate of authenticity
    - Instruction manual
    - Original purchase receipt
    - Service records

    <Warning>
      Store box and papers separately from watch for security
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Update Market Values" icon="chart-line">
    Watch values fluctuate:
    - Check comparable sales on Chrono24, eBay, authorized dealers
    - Update market value periodically (annually or semi-annually)
    - Track market trends for your models
    - Consider condition when valuing

    **Factors affecting value:**
    - Brand and model popularity
    - Condition
    - Box and papers completeness
    - Service history
    - Rarity
    - Market demand
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Organize by Collection" icon="folder">
    Use Storehouses collections to organize:
    - **By brand:** "Rolex Watches", "Omega Collection"
    - **By type:** "Dive Watches", "Dress Watches", "Chronographs"
    - **By storage:** "Watch Box A", "Safe", "Wearing Rotation"
    - **By purpose:** "Investment Pieces", "Daily Wearers"
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Set Accurate Locations" icon="location-dot">
    Track where watches are stored:
    - Watch box/safe at home
    - Bank safe deposit box
    - Watch winder
    - Currently being worn
    - With watchmaker for service

    **Benefits:**
    - Quick retrieval
    - Insurance documentation
    - Service scheduling
    - Rotation planning
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Insurance Considerations" icon="shield">
    For valuable watches:
    - Get specialized watch insurance or valuable items rider
    - Document with photos and appraisals
    - Update insurance values annually
    - Keep records of purchase and service

    <Tip>
      Export your watch inventory from Storehouses for insurance documentation
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Watch Maintenance Tips

<Card title="Keep Watches Running Well" icon="gear">
  **For Automatic Watches:**
  - Wear regularly or use watch winder
  - Service every 5-10 years (brand dependent)
  - Avoid magnetization (keep away from strong magnets)
  - Have water resistance checked during service

  **For Manual Watches:**
  - Wind daily at same time
  - Don't overwind
  - Regular servicing important
  - Handle crown gently

  **For All Watches:**
  - Store in cool, dry place
  - Avoid extreme temperatures
  - Clean regularly with soft cloth
  - Check bracelet screws periodically
</Card>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Where do I find my reference number?" icon="hashtag">
    **Common locations:**
    - **Rolex:** Between lugs (12 o'clock side) or on warranty card
    - **Omega:** Case back or warranty card
    - **Patek Philippe:** Case back or warranty card
    - **Most brands:** Papers/warranty card or engraved on case back

    If you can't find it, check your purchase documents or contact an authorized dealer.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Should I track each watch separately?" icon="watch">
    **Yes**, create a separate entry for each watch, even if same model:
    - Each has unique serial number
    - Different purchase dates/prices
    - Individual service histories
    - Separate condition tracking
    - Better for insurance documentation
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I value my watch?" icon="dollar-sign">
    **Research current market value:**
    1. Check Chrono24.com for comparable listings
    2. Review completed eBay auctions
    3. Contact authorized dealers
    4. Consider professional appraisal for high-value pieces

    **Factors affecting value:**
    - Box and papers (adds 10-30%)
    - Condition (excellent vs. good)
    - Service history (recent service adds value)
    - Market demand and trends
    - Rarity and production numbers
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="When should I service my watch?" icon="wrench">
    **General guidelines:**
    - **Rolex:** Every 10 years (newer models), 5 years (older)
    - **Omega:** Every 5-8 years
    - **Other mechanical:** Every 3-5 years
    - **Quartz:** Battery every 2-3 years, service every 10 years

    **Service immediately if:**
    - Time keeping becomes inaccurate
    - Crown feels loose or difficult
    - Water inside the crystal
    - Any unusual sounds
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Box and papers - how important?" icon="box">
    **Very important for resale value:**
    - Full set: 100% value
    - No box/papers: Typically 10-30% lower value
    - Papers only: Better than nothing (proves authenticity)
    - Box only: Minimal impact on value

    **Most important paper:** Warranty card with serial number match

    <Tip>
      Always request box and papers when purchasing pre-owned
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How to photograph my watch for documentation?" icon="camera">
    **Essential shots:**
    1. Full watch face (straight-on, well-lit)
    2. Side profile showing case thickness
    3. Case back
    4. Between lugs showing serial number (if accessible)
    5. Clasp and bracelet
    6. Any damage or wear
    7. Box, papers, and warranty card
    8. Movement (if exhibition case back)

    **Photography tips:**
    - Natural lighting or bright white light
    - Clean watch before photographing
    - Plain background (white or black)
    - Sharp, in-focus images
    - Multiple angles for complete documentation
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
