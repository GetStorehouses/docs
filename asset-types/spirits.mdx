## Overview

Track your spirits collection with detailed fields for distillery, age statements, ABV, bottle numbers, and limited editions. Perfect for whiskey collectors, bourbon enthusiasts, and rare spirits investors.

<Card title="Spirits Tracking Features" icon="whiskey-glass">
  - Spirit type and category
  - Distillery and brand tracking
  - Age statements
  - ABV (alcohol by volume)
  - Limited editions and bottle numbers
  - Cask information
  - Provenance and purchase history
  - Collection organization
</Card>

### Age and Specifications

<ParamField path="age_statement" type="string">
  Age statement or maturation period

  **Format examples:**
  - "18 Year"
  - "12 Years Old"
  - "25 Year Old"
  - "NAS" (No Age Statement)
  - "21 Años"

  **Multiple ages:**
  - Some blends: "Aged 12-25 Years"
  - Solera systems: "Solera 23"

  **Important notes:**
  - Age = time in barrel (not bottle)
  - Younger for some spirits (rum, tequila)
  - NAS can be excellent (focus on quality)

  <Tip>
    Age doesn't always equal quality, but affects value significantly
  </Tip>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="abv" type="number">
  Alcohol by volume (percentage)

  **Examples:**
  - 40.0 (standard strength)
  - 43.0 (common premium)
  - 46.0 (non-chill filtered common)
  - 50.0 (100 proof)
  - 57.1 (cask strength example)
  - 60.5 (barrel proof example)

  **Categories:**
  - 40-43%: Standard strength
  - 45-50%: Higher proof
  - 50-60%+: Cask/barrel strength

  <Note>
    Higher ABV often indicates cask strength or barrel proof - no water added
  </Note>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="proof" type="number">
  Proof (U.S. standard)

  **Conversion:** Proof = ABV × 2

  **Examples:**
  - 80 proof = 40% ABV
  - 90 proof = 45% ABV
  - 100 proof = 50% ABV (Bottled in Bond)
  - 120 proof = 60% ABV

  <Tip>
    Either ABV or proof is sufficient - they convert directly
  </Tip>
</ParamField>

</ParamField>

### Cask and Maturation

<ParamField path="cask_type" type="string">
  Cask type or finish

  **Common casks:**
  - **Bourbon Cask** (Ex-bourbon barrel)
  - **Sherry Cask** (Oloroso, Pedro Ximénez)
  - **Port Cask**
  - **Wine Cask** (Sauternes, Bordeaux)
  - **Rum Cask**
  - **Virgin Oak** (New charred oak)
  - **Mizunara Oak** (Japanese oak)

  **Multiple casks:**
  - "Double Cask" (matured in two cask types)
  - "Triple Cask"
  - "Finished in [type]" (e.g., "Sherry Finish")

  **Example:**
  - "Matured in Ex-Bourbon and Oloroso Sherry Casks"
  - "PX Sherry Cask Finish"

  <Tip>
    Cask type significantly affects flavor profile and collector interest
  </Tip>
</ParamField>

### Packaging and Condition

<ParamField path="bottle_size" type="string">
  Bottle size

  **Standard sizes:**
  - **Miniature** (50ml)
  - **Half Bottle** (375ml)
  - **Standard** (750ml) - Most common
  - **Liter** (1000ml)
  - **Magnum** (1500ml) - Rare

  **Note:** Most spirits sold in 750ml
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="packaging" type="string">
  Original packaging included

  **Options:**
  - **Full Set** - Bottle, box, certificate, everything
  - **Box and Certificate**
  - **Box Only**
  - **Bottle Only**

  **Luxury packaging:**
  - Wooden presentation box
  - Leather case
  - Crystal decanter
  - Display case
  - Certificate of authenticity

  <Note>
    Original packaging, especially for rare releases, adds significant value
  </Note>
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="bottle_condition" type="string">
  Condition of bottle and contents

  **Bottle:**
  - Pristine (perfect, sealed)
  - Excellent (minimal wear)
  - Very Good (light wear)
  - Good (some wear, intact)

  **Fill level:**
  - Full (perfect)
  - High Shoulder
  - Mid Shoulder (concern for old bottles)

  **Seal:**
  - Intact/Sealed
  - Broken/Opened
  - Resealed

  <Warning>
    Opened bottles lose most collector value but can still be tracked for personal inventory
  </Warning>
</ParamField>

  - "Purchased from distillery visitor center May 2023"
  - "Won in state lottery allocation 2022"

## Spirit Categories

### Whiskey

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Scotch Whisky" icon="flag">
    **Single Malt:**
    - One distillery, malted barley only
    - Regional characteristics
    - Age statements common
    - Most collectible category

    **Regions:**
    - **Islay:** Peated, smoky (Lagavulin, Ardbeg, Laphroaig)
    - **Speyside:** Elegant, fruity (Macallan, Glenfiddich, Balvenie)
    - **Highland:** Diverse (Dalmore, Glenmorangie)
    - **Lowland:** Light, floral (Auchentoshan)

    **Investment:** Older expressions (18+, 25+, 30+ year)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Bourbon" icon="flag-usa">
    **Requirements:**
    - 51%+ corn in mash bill
    - Aged in new charred oak barrels
    - Produced in USA (mostly Kentucky)

    **Allocated/Rare:**
    - Pappy Van Winkle (all ages)
    - Buffalo Trace Antique Collection (BTAC)
    - E.H. Taylor lineup
    - William Larue Weller
    - George T. Stagg

    **Single Barrel:** Often store picks, unique flavor profiles

    **Bottled in Bond:** 100 proof, 4+ years, single distillery/season
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Rye Whiskey" icon="wheat">
    - 51%+ rye grain
    - Spicier, drier than bourbon
    - Growing collector interest

    **Notable:** WhistlePig, Thomas H. Handy (BTAC), Michter's
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Japanese Whisky" icon="mountain">
    **Top distilleries:**
    - Yamazaki, Hakushu (Suntory)
    - Yoichi, Miyagikyo (Nikka)
    - Hibiki (blend)

    **Characteristics:**
    - Influenced by Scotch tradition
    - Refined, delicate flavors
    - Limited availability
    - Premium pricing

    **Investment:** Age-stated Yamazaki, limited editions
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Irish Whiskey" icon="clover">
    - Triple distilled (typically)
    - Smooth, approachable
    - Growing premium segment

    **Notable:** Redbreast, Green Spot, Midleton Very Rare
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Brandy

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Cognac" icon="grapes">
    **Designations (age):**
    - **VS** (Very Special) - Minimum 2 years
    - **VSOP** (Very Superior Old Pale) - Minimum 4 years
    - **XO** (Extra Old) - Minimum 10 years
    - **XXO** - Minimum 14 years

    **Top houses:**
    - Hennessy, Rémy Martin, Martell, Courvoisier
    - Louis XIII (extremely premium)

    **Grades:**
    - Grande Champagne (finest)
    - Petite Champagne
    - Borderies, Fins Bois
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Armagnac" icon="wine-glass">
    - Older tradition than Cognac
    - Single distillation
    - Often vintage dated
    - Excellent value vs. Cognac
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Document Provenance" icon="file-text">
    **Why provenance matters:**
    - Proves authenticity (counterfeits exist)
    - Documents allocation/lottery wins
    - Increases resale value
    - Important for rare bottles

    **What to document:**
    - Purchase location and date
    - Receipt/invoice
    - Lottery confirmation (if applicable)
    - Store pick details
    - Original owner (if secondary)

    **Upload to Storehouses:**
    - Purchase receipts
    - Allocation confirmations
    - Certificates of authenticity
    - Photos of purchase/allocation

    <Tip>
      For allocated bottles (Pappy, BTAC), save lottery confirmation and original receipt
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Store Properly" icon="temperature-half">
    **Optimal storage:**
    - **Upright** (not on side like wine)
    - **Cool temperature** (60-70°F)
    - **Away from sunlight** (UV degrades spirits)
    - **Stable temperature** (avoid fluctuations)
    - **Low humidity** (preserves labels)

    **Why upright:**
    - High-proof spirits degrade corks
    - Prevents cork taint
    - Preserves seal integrity

    **Long-term storage:**
    - Consider parafilm on cork/cap seal
    - Climate-controlled space
    - Away from heat sources

    <Warning>
      Direct sunlight and heat can degrade spirits and fade labels
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Photograph Thoroughly" icon="camera">
    **Essential photos:**
    - Front label (clear, readable)
    - Back label (proof, details)
    - Top of bottle (seal condition)
    - Bottle number (if numbered)
    - Original box/packaging
    - Certificate of authenticity
    - Fill level (for old bottles)
    - Any damage or issues

    **For investment bottles:**
    - Multiple angles
    - Close-ups of important details
    - Serial/bottle numbers
    - Proof of provenance
    - Seal condition

    <Tip>
      Photograph upon receipt - documents condition at purchase
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Track Market Values" icon="chart-line">
    **Resources for valuation:**
    - Secondary market (Facebook groups, Reddit)
    - Auction results (Sotheby's, Acker)
    - Online retailers (Caskers, ReserveBar)
    - Local stores (for current retail)

    **High-value categories:**
    - Pappy Van Winkle (all ages)
    - Buffalo Trace Antique Collection
    - Japanese whisky (age-stated)
    - Old/rare Scotch (25+ years)
    - Single barrels/limited editions

    **Update regularly:**
    - Allocated bottles can appreciate quickly
    - Annual updates for collection
    - Before selling

    <Note>
      Secondary market prices can be 10-20x retail for allocated bourbon
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Organize Your Collection" icon="folder">
    **Use Storehouses collections:**
    - **By type:** "Bourbon", "Scotch", "Japanese Whisky"
    - **By status:** "Sealed Investment", "Open for Drinking"
    - **By value:** "Daily Drinkers", "Special Occasions", "Investment"
    - **By distillery:** "Buffalo Trace Products", "Macallan"
    - **By storage:** "Home Bar", "Cellar", "Safe"

    **Track location:**
    - Specific shelf or cabinet
    - Home bar vs. storage
    - Easy retrieval

    <Tip>
      Separate investment bottles from drinking bottles in your organization
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Insurance for Valuable Bottles" icon="shield">
    **Consider insurance for:**
    - Bottles over $500-1,000
    - Allocated bottles (Pappy, BTAC)
    - Collections over $10,000
    - Rare/discontinued expressions

    **Coverage options:**
    - Valuable articles rider (homeowner's)
    - Collectibles insurance
    - Specific spirits insurance

    **Documentation needed:**
    - Purchase receipts
    - Photos of bottles
    - Current market values
    - Provenance documentation
    - Storehouses export

    <Note>
      Market value often much higher than purchase price for allocated bottles
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Handle Opened Bottles" icon="bottle-droplet">
    **Once opened:**
    - Value drops significantly (not "collectible")
    - Still useful to track for personal inventory
    - Update status in Storehouses

    **Preservation:**
    - Consume within 1-2 years for best quality
    - Store upright, sealed
    - Keep away from light and heat
    - Consider smaller bottles for long-term (reduce oxidation)

    **Note in Storehouses:**
    - Update bottle condition to "Opened"
    - Adjust value (personal consumption value)
    - Track fill level if monitoring consumption

    <Warning>
      Never represent opened bottles as sealed if selling - this is fraud
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Investment Considerations

<Card title="Spirits as Investments" icon="chart-line">

  - Pappy Van Winkle (all ages)

  - Rarity and limited availability

  - Market can be volatile

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I know if my bottle is valuable?" icon="dollar-sign">
    **Check these factors:**
    - **Rarity:** Limited releases, discontinued
    - **Allocation:** Was it allocated/lottery item?
    - **Age:** Older age statements more valuable
    - **Distillery:** Prestigious names command premiums
    - **Condition:** Sealed, pristine condition essential

    **Research value:**
    - Secondary market groups (Facebook, Reddit)
    - Completed auction results
    - Online spirits retailers
    - Collector communities

    **Most valuable:**
    - Pappy Van Winkle (any age)
    - BTAC releases
    - Old Japanese whisky (Yamazaki 25+)
    - Very old Scotch (30+ years)
    - Rare single barrels
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Should I open or keep sealed?" icon="bottle">
    **Keep sealed if:**
    - Rare/allocated bottle
    - Investment purpose
    - High secondary market value
    - You have duplicates (drink one, keep one)

    **Open if:**
    - Want to enjoy it (spirits made for drinking!)
    - Not particularly rare
    - Have multiple bottles
    - Personal consumption collection

    **Middle ground:**
    - Buy two: one to drink, one to keep
    - Open lower-value bottles
    - Keep rare/allocated sealed

    <Note>
      Remember: spirits are made to be enjoyed. Balance collecting with drinking.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How should I store spirits long-term?" icon="box">
    **Best practices:**
    - **Upright** (always, never on side)
    - Cool (60-70°F), stable temperature
    - Dark (away from sunlight/UV)
    - Low humidity (preserves labels)
    - Secure location

    **For very long-term:**
    - Parafilm around cap/cork seal
    - Original box if available
    - Climate-controlled space
    - Inventory tracking

    **Avoid:**
    - Direct sunlight
    - Temperature extremes
    - High humidity (damages labels)
    - Storing on side (damages cork)

    <Warning>
      Poor storage can damage seals and degrade spirits over decades
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are there counterfeit spirits?" icon="shield-exclamation">
    **Yes, counterfeits exist, especially for:**
    - Pappy Van Winkle
    - Buffalo Trace Antique Collection
    - Old rare Scotch
    - Japanese whisky
    - High-value bottles

    **Protection:**
    - Buy from reputable sources only
    - Check seals and labels carefully
    - Verify bottle details (weight, glass, printing)
    - Document provenance
    - Be wary of "too good to be true" deals

    **Red flags:**
    - Suspiciously low prices
    - Private sales without provenance
    - Poor label quality
    - Incorrect details (proof, age, etc.)
    - Seller reluctance to provide details

    <Warning>
      Only buy rare/expensive bottles from authorized retailers, reputable auctions, or proven collectors
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can spirits go bad?" icon="question">
    **Short answer:** Sealed spirits don't spoil

    **Unopened bottles:**
    - Stable indefinitely if stored properly
    - High alcohol content preserves liquid
    - May lose some volume over decades (evaporation)
    - Corks can dry/degrade (very old bottles)

    **Opened bottles:**
    - Oxidation begins
    - Best consumed within 1-2 years
    - Won't "spoil" but flavor degrades
    - Low-proof spirits (liqueurs) more susceptible

    **Issues:**
    - Cork disintegration (very old bottles)
    - Evaporation through seal (decades)
    - Label degradation (cosmetic only)

    <Note>
      Unlike wine, spirits don't mature in bottle - won't improve over time
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What about bottle numbers - do they matter?" icon="hashtag">
    **Low numbers more valuable:**
    - Bottle #1 - significant premium
    - Bottles #1-100 - modest premium
    - Bottles #1-10 - noticeable premium

    **Other special numbers:**
    - #007 (for Bond fans)
    - Matching sets (same number, different bottles)
    - Last bottle in series

    **When it matters:**
    - Single barrel releases
    - Small batch limited editions
    - Commemorative releases

    **When it doesn't:**
    - Very large releases (10,000+ bottles)
    - Standard age-stated releases
